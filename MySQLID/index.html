MySQL Identity: A SQL Mystery Game

The Challenge

Instructional Problem

Technical skills like SQL are in high demand, but traditional learning methods may fail to provide learners with immediate, hands-on practice. This can lead to low engagement and difficulty in applying concepts to real-world problems. The challenge was to create a learning experience that makes SQL fundamentals not just understandable, but genuinely engaging and memorable.

The Learner Persona

The target audience consists of beginners to SQL, such as aspiring data analysts, marketing professionals, or students who need to grasp foundational database concepts. They are motivated to learn a practical skill but are often intimidated by coding. They need a low-stakes, interactive environment where they can experiment, make mistakes, and learn by doing without the pressure of a live database.

The Solution & My Process

I designed and developed "MySQL Identity," an immersive, narrative-driven game that teaches fundamental SQL concepts. The solution was conceptualized and built in a matter of hours by partnering with Google's Gemini. My role was to act as the Instructional Designer and Project Lead, guiding the AI through strategic prompts to translate my educational vision into a fully functional application.

Key Features & Skills Demonstrated

Instructional Design & Gamification

Applied gamification principles by embedding learning objectives within a compelling thriller narrative. The game uses timers, penalties, and a strong storyline to increase learner motivation and engagement.

AI-Powered Rapid Prototyping

Demonstrated the ability to move from concept to a fully functional prototype at high speed by using AI as a development partner. This process involved strategic prompting, iteration, and validation.

Dynamic Assessment

Engineered a validation system that checks the results of a query, not just the syntax. This rewards true understanding and allows for multiple correct approaches to solving a problem.

Socratic Feedback Mechanism

Designed and prompted the AI to provide feedback using the Socratic method. Instead of giving answers, it asks guiding questions to foster critical thinking and help learners discover solutions on their own.

Adaptive Scaffolding

Implemented a multi-layered support system, including interactive quizzes for common errors, a progressive hint system, and a final "reveal answer" option with a penalty to cater to different learner needs.

Technical Proficiency

Managed the entire development lifecycle, including troubleshooting and refining the AI-generated HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to ensure a functional, polished, and pedagogically sound final product.

Measuring Success

Proposed Evaluation Plan

Engagement & Motivation:

"Do learners find the gamified approach more engaging and motivating than traditional SQL tutorials?"

Measured via: A post-game survey asking learners to rate their engagement, the clarity of the story, and the helpfulness of the feedback system.

Learning Outcome:

"Can learners correctly apply fundamental SQL concepts to solve problems within the game?"

Measured via: Analyzing in-game data, such as task completion rates, number of attempts per task, and usage of the hint system to assess skill acquisition and identify common struggle points.

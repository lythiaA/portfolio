Interactive E-Learning Simulation

Leading With Impact: Running Effective Virtual Meetings

The Challenge

Instructional Problem

In today's hybrid work environment at a leading global logistics firm, leading effective virtual meetings is a critical skill. However, leaders often face challenges with keeping teams engaged, using communication tools appropriately, and ensuring meetings are productive from start to finish. The goal was to move leaders beyond basic knowledge and empower them to strategically analyze and overcome these common hurdles.

The Learner Persona

The target audience is mid- to senior-level professionals at the company. These leaders are expected to make strategic decisions and need practical training that helps them translate theory into confident action during both internal team meetings and key customer interactions.

The Solution & My Process

I designed an immersive, scenario-based e-learning module that simulates the entire lifecycle of a virtual meeting. The module is structured around three key phases: Pre-Meeting Planning, Leading the Meeting, and Follow-Up. Through a series of interactive activities and a summative branching scenario, learners practice making critical decisions in a safe environment, receiving immediate, constructive feedback to guide their development.

This module was created using Articulate Storyline 360 to build a polished and highly interactive experience. To create dynamic and engaging video content for the scenarios, I leveraged AI-powered tools for video generation.

Key Features & Skills Demonstrated

Instructional Design

Designed a logical, scaffolded learning path that follows the natural progression of a meeting. The module builds skills progressively, ensuring learners have a clear mental model from start to finish.

Immersive Scenarios

Utilized a summative branching video scenario where learners face realistic meeting challenges. This approach promotes critical thinking and allows for safe learning from mistakes through constructive feedback.

Engaging Interactivity

Integrated various interactive elements, including drag-and-drop rankings, tool selection activities, and clickable infographics to keep the learning dynamic and interesting.

UI/UX & Visual Design

Created a clean, professional interface using a clear visual hierarchy, ample white space, and consistent alignment to enhance readability and reduce cognitive load for the learner.

Universal Design (UDL)

Presented content in multiple formats (voiceover, text, video, graphics) to cater to diverse learning preferences. Learners can demonstrate understanding through varied actions like clicking and selecting paths.

Accessibility (POUR)

Ensured the module was perceivable with closed captions for all audio, operable via keyboard, and understandable through clear instructions and a consistent interface.

Measuring Success

Proposed Evaluation Plan

Confidence and Engagement:

"Do learners feel more prepared and confident in their ability to lead effective virtual meetings after the training?"

Measured via: A post-module survey asking learners to rate their confidence levels and the relevance of the scenarios to their real-world jobs.

Learning Outcome:

"Can learners successfully analyze a meeting scenario and select the appropriate tools and techniques to improve it?"

Measured via: Analyzing performance data from the summative scenario and interactive quizzes to assess decision-making accuracy.

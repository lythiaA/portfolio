The Challenge

Instructional Problem

New specialists in a remote onboarding program understood the technical aspects of their job but lacked the confidence and consultative skills to lead effective 1:1 client sessions. The existing training focused on the "what," not the "how," leading to anxiety and inconsistent performance.

The Learner: "Alex"

The solution was designed for "Alex," a motivated but overwhelmed new hire. Alex needs a sense of psychological safety to learn without fear of judgment and prefers practical, hands-on activities. Their primary challenge is translating abstract knowledge into confident, client-facing action.

The Solution & My Process

I designed and developed a comprehensive, asynchronous e-learning module in Canvas using a "Practice, Review, Perform" model. The design was grounded in a literature review, applying principles of Bandura's Social Cognitive Theory to build self-efficacy and Authentic Learning to ensure skills would transfer to the job.

Key Features & Skills Demonstrated

Learner-Centered Design

The solution was based on a detailed learner analysis, persona, and empathy map to address specific user needs and anxieties.

Authentic Assessment

Used a video-based simulation for the practice and assessment of real-world facilitation skills in a low-stakes environment.

Structured Peer Feedback

Designed a detailed rubric and a structured peer review process within Canvas to provide formative feedback and build confidence.

Data-Driven Evaluation

Integrated pre/post surveys and rubric scores to collect quantitative and qualitative data on the module's impact.

Custom Development (LMS)

Used custom HTML and inline CSS to create a polished, accessible, and user-friendly interface within the constraints of the Canvas LMS.

Quality Standards

The module was designed in accordance with the OSCQR rubric, ensuring a high-quality, accessible, and learner-centered experience.

Measuring Success

Research Questions

Learner Experience:

"How does the experience of evaluating a peer's performance... affect a new specialist's self-efficacy...?"

Measured via: A pre/post ungraded survey, analyzing the change in learners' responses on a 5-point Likert scale.

Learning Outcome:

"How does a peer review activity impact a new specialist's ability to verbally facilitate a solution...?"

Measured via: Scoring the initial and final video submissions against a 9-point analytic rubric to quantify performance improvement.
